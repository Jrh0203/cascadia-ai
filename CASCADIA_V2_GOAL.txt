R2-MAP EXPERT-ITERATION CONTRACT

Implement and test the width-192 exact-R2 fixed-latent Multitask Action Perceiver described in `docs/v2/R2_MAP_EXPERT_ITERATION_RESEARCH_PLAN.md`. The detailed plan is subordinate to this goal. When it contains stale topology or storage language, repair it before executing that section.

The scientific baseline has these invariants:

- Begin with exactly 100,000 deterministic four-player greedy games, expressed as topology-free logical shards with disjoint seed ranges and scheduled across the john1-john3 fabric.
- Project each bootstrap game to exactly one deterministic focal seat (`global_game_index mod 4`, 20 decisions) for training. The complete four-seat replay remains authoritative for targets and audits, but correlated all-greedy seat copies are not repeated as training rows.
- Bootstrap is selected-action value training, not greedy-policy imitation: train score-to-go, score components, public market decisions, opponent-action, and market-survival heads on every focal decision; freeze the bootstrap-policy loss at zero while retaining its architecture/schema for later expert iterations. Inference continues to enumerate every exact legal action and ranks by `exact_afterstate_score + predicted_score_to_go`; no inference action is pruned.
- Feed native MLX through a persistent, backpressured Rust packed-batch pipe. The producer emits one exact no-pruning batch at a time, waits for an identity acknowledgement, and checkpoints the source/game/turn/batch cursor and sampler identity. Expanded `.r2map` training windows and caches are forbidden.
- Run the initial bootstrap for exactly one focal-seat epoch. Additional exposure is authorized only after held-out whole-game validation improves and a new schedule is recorded. Before a production launch, a 100-real-step end-to-end gate must measure feeder plus MLX throughput, memory, swap, loss, resume, and projected completion wall time; a multi-day projection blocks launch.
- Train the initial R2-MAP checkpoint from scratch on john1 with native MLX/Metal.
- Ignore MCE for this campaign. Do not prune, approximate, quantize, or omit legal moves. Optimize performance only after the exact implementation is correct and tested.
- Every iterative self-play game has exactly one seat controlled by the newest promoted checkpoint. The other three seats use locally frozen greedy or earlier promoted opponents; the newest checkpoint never occupies two seats.
- Train each candidate only from the newest completed generation round. Historical checkpoints are opponent-pool members, not historical training examples.
- Use exploration noise during training self-play and decrease it through a preregistered schedule as iterations mature. Benchmark and promotion games remain deterministic.
- Each generation round is one approximately 45-minute wall-clock self-play window across all three hosts. Hosts run independently from frozen local inputs and disjoint seeds; no per-game or per-move coordination is permitted.
- When generation ends, all hosts stop, checksum their completed shards, and return them to john1. John1 verifies and installs the complete round before training begins.
- During john1 MLX training, the fabric admits only disjoint benchmarks of the previously promoted checkpoint, one iteration behind, and reserves john1 from competing container work. No node generates the next round.
- After training and checkpoint verification, submit every paired candidate comparison as its own topology-free `pair-NNNN` work item. Bacalhau alone owns placement, admission, retry, and rescheduling. Only a promoted candidate may become the single newest-model seat for the next generation window.
- Repeat the generate, train/benchmark, gate, and promote loop until the research evidence supports stopping or the 100-point goal is reached.

Every training run must emit monotonic loss events and robust atomic checkpoints containing model, optimizer, scheduler, RNG, dataset identity, source/image identity, and validation state. A checkpoint must reload independently and resume without changing completed work. Preserve the incumbent, every promoted checkpoint, `last_verified`, and at least the two newest complete recovery points.

Every longitudinal benchmark and candidate gate reports sample count, mean, standard deviation, standard error, 95% confidence interval, P10/P50/P90, min/max, exact score histogram, paired delta, win/tie/loss counts where applicable, throughput, latency, memory, and swap. Report score anatomy for each animal and all animals in aggregate; each terrain and all terrain in aggregate; nature tokens; and Pinecone/wild-token acquisition, spending, and scoring. Use paired, held-out, leakage-proof seeds and fixed seat rotation.

PRODUCT REQUIREMENTS

1. A Correct, Fast Rules Engine

- Exact Cascadia rules for 1-4 players.
- AAAAA as the primary research configuration.
- Habitat bonuses configurable and excluded from the primary 100-point metric.
- All wildlife scoring variants supported if they can be verified.
- Correct market overflow replacement, mulligans, independent drafting, nature tokens, bag behavior, rotations, and habitat connectivity.
- Deterministic seeded simulation.
- Efficient apply/undo or immutable transitions suitable for search.
- Stable serialization and hashing.
- Property tests, rule fixtures, regression tests, and scoring tests.
- Clear separation between rules, simulation, search, models, training, evaluation, and presentation.

2. A Clean AI Platform

- A small, coherent set of production strategies.
- A clearly separated experimental research layer.
- Typed configuration instead of scattered environment-variable behavior.
- One canonical move representation and legal-move generator.
- One canonical benchmark protocol.
- Profiling and benchmark tools that measure move generation, scoring, inference, search, and complete games.
- Reproducible seeded comparisons with paired trials and confidence intervals.
- No reliance on historical weight files as the final solution.

3. MLX-Native Neural Research

- Use MLX for neural training and Apple GPU computation.
- Build a clean dataset format with schema versions, metadata, validation, checksums, and streaming support.
- Implement resumable collection and training.
- Support train/validation/test seed partitions to prevent benchmark leakage.
- Investigate architectures from first principles, including:
  - compact spatial hex networks,
  - residual HexCNNs,
  - entity/set encoders for market, bags, opponents, and habitat races,
  - policy and decomposed value heads,
  - distributional or uncertainty-aware values where justified,
  - teacher distillation,
  - self-play and search-guided policy iteration.
- Choose architectures through measured accuracy, ranking quality, inference latency, calibration, and playing strength.
- Keep Rust and MLX boundaries explicit and well documented.
- Keep the Linux-container CPU/reference boundary and the native john1 Metal boundary explicit; checkpoint and tensor parity must be verified across them before promotion.
- Avoid redundant Rust implementations of neural training.
- Provide one-command local training, resumption, evaluation, and checkpoint promotion.

- random,
