## The 121 Versus 441 Question Is Resolved

John specifically suspected that 441 cells were unnecessary and that roughly
121 cells should be sufficient. This was verified carefully rather than
assumed.

The exact conclusion is:

- The historical 441-cell square is materially oversized.
- There is no regular centered hex disk with exactly 121 cells.
- A centered radius-5 disk has 91 cells.
- A centered radius-6 disk has 127 cells.
- Recentered radius 6 retained every measured generated event.
- Recentered radius 5 retained every open graded-corpus occupied, frontier,
  selected-destination, and complete-candidate event.
- Legal adversarial boards can overflow radius 6, so any fixed disk requires
  an exact overflow path.
- The preferred V2 substrate is not a dense 127-cell tensor. It is exact
  sparse R2 occupied/frontier/component/motif state.
- The only remaining 441-coordinate dependency in recent T1 work is sparse
  legacy NNUE feature-index compatibility. No dense 441-cell V2 tensor is
  allocated, trained, hashed, or searched.

Canonical evidence:

- docs/v2/reports/state-footprint-census-v1-result.md
- docs/v2/decisions/0128-state-footprint-census.md
- docs/v2/decisions/0135-r0-lossless-spatial-representation-contract.md

- docs/v2/decisions/0146-r2-sparse-mlx-architecture-tournament.md

opponent-policy error, and search allocation, but it is enough to block
promotion of this stack.

## Recommended Next Research Direction

The next primary hypothesis should be O2 demand-supply matching.

Why O2:

- direct compact R2 ranking beat every legacy-leaf/search treatment
- O1 showed that future access matters behaviorally but direct probability
  fusion was too weak
- exact semantic supply showed a real but uneven signal
- current models do not explicitly solve whether a board opportunity can be
  satisfied by market or hidden supply before opponents consume it
- O2 targets the action-relevant missing relation without requiring T2

O2 hypothesis:

    A board opportunity is valuable only when compatible current-market or
    unseen-supply objects can satisfy it before opponents consume them.

Represent the state as a bipartite opportunity graph:

- demand side: open wildlife-pattern needs, legal receiving cells, habitat
  growth boundaries, token-use needs, and completion deadlines
- supply side: current market tile/wildlife pairs, independent draft options,
  exact semantic unseen counts, expected arrivals, and seat-relative access
- edges: compatibility, placement legality, arrival probability, access
  timing, competition, opportunity cost, and expected completion value

The first O2 experiment should be a foundation/identifiability campaign, not
an immediate large neural sweep:

1. Define exact demand and supply objects with canonical IDs.
2. Build a deterministic small exact matching or min-cost-flow teacher.
3. Prove D6, replay, serialization, target-independence, and no-hidden-state
   invariants.
4. Audit whether exact matching explains residual R4800 regret beyond the
   current R2 ranker.
5. Measure signal by phase, scarcity, independent-draft availability, and
   wildlife family.
6. Only if the exact teacher has material incremental signal, run matched MLX
   arms such as attention-only, Sinkhorn-relaxed matching, and exact-teacher
   auxiliary supervision.

Suggested matched MLX comparison after the foundation passes:

- B0: frozen exact-R2 control
- B1: deterministic matching summaries
- B2: learned cross-attention over demand and supply objects
- B3: Sinkhorn or assignment-consistency auxiliary head

Freeze all effect sizes and protected-slice guardrails before training. A
reasonable starting requirement is at least 0.05 lower retained R4800 regret,
a paired game-bootstrap interval wholly below zero, no top-1 or pairwise
regression, and no collapse in independent-draft or scarce-supply states.
Those are recommendations, not yet a preregistration.

- john1: exact O2 schema, invariant tests, and aggregation
- john2: demand/supply corpus extraction or exact matching teacher
- john3: residual-signal and protected-slice audit
- john4: performance/resource probe or an independently useful O3 semantic
  census while O2 prerequisites run

Do not fill idle machines with duplicate training. One trainer is enough
unless seed sensitivity or cross-host replication is preregistered.

Secondary directions:

## Operational Caveat: macOS Child Reaping

During the T1 implementation, Codex.app accumulated roughly 1,600 defunct
children and new processes began failing with os error 35. Sending SIGCHLD to
the Codex desktop parent caused it to reap children without terminating the
app or workloads.

If process creation becomes strangely slow or fails:

    ps -axo state= | awk '$1 ~ /^Z/ {n++} END {print n+0}'
    ps -axo ppid=,state= | awk '$2 ~ /^Z/ {count[$1]++} END {for (p in count) print count[p], p}'
    ps -axo pid=,ppid=,command= | rg 'Codex.app/Contents/MacOS/Codex'

Then, only after identifying the current Codex PID:

    kill -CHLD CODEX_PID

Do not reuse the old PID blindly. Do not kill the Codex process. This is a
desktop child-reaping issue, not a repository defect. At handoff only two
zombies were present.

For long shell-managed Python or SSH commands, using exec as the shell's final
command reduced extra process layers:

    exec .venv/bin/python ...
    exec ssh john2 ...

Release Cargo builds should use -j 1 when the host is under process pressure.

## Research Discipline

Every experiment should have:

- one falsifiable hypothesis
- a preregistration before treatment results
- explicit data partition and claim boundary
- immutable source/data/model identities
- matched controls
- deterministic seeds or registered stochastic domains
- effect-size threshold, uncertainty gate, and protected-slice guardrails
- exact primary/replay expectations where reproducibility matters
- resource envelope and stop rule
- queue graph and artifact path
- terminal passed, failed, inconclusive, or invalid classification
- ADR and result report
- experiment-ledger update visible on the dashboard

Invalid and failed are different:

- invalid means the protocol or implementation cannot answer the question
- failed means the protocol worked and the treatment did not pass

Never relabel a clean null as a pipeline failure. Never promote a directional
point estimate that missed its frozen gate.

Use R600/R1200/R4800 teacher labels only under their registered contracts.
Keep train, open validation, and sealed test separate. Do not inspect the
sealed test to choose an architecture or threshold.
