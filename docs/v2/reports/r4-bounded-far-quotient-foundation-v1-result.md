independent quotient hypotheses retained every registered distinction and

Q1 is the preregistered minimal successor. Q2 is the richest passing
successor because Q4 missed the frozen P99 limit of 192 tokens. Q3 also
advances to the matched learned comparison.

The result resolves ADR 0154's narrow compactness failure without changing
the failed threshold, deleting exact state, or hiding cardinality inside an
unmeasured vector. The accepted 61-cell focal field, all far habitat and
wildlife components, exact overflow sidecar, and deterministic quotient
summaries fit within the serving envelope. The learned round is now
authorized, but no representation has yet earned promotion.

## The 441 And 121 Claims

This experiment strengthens the earlier geometric finding:

- the historical 441-cell square is not needed;
- 121 is not the size of a complete centered hex disk;
- the exact regular capacities are 61 cells at radius four, 91 at radius
  five, and 127 at radius six; and
- a 61-cell focal field plus exact overflow is sufficient as the shared
  substrate for all three passing bounded views.

The performance gain here is not from pretending that the board ends at 61
cells. Every out-of-field coordinate remains authoritative in `CSR4AM1`.
Only the model-visible far projection is compressed.

## Q4 Negative Evidence

Q4 retained up to 16 exact wildlife motif buckets and 24 exact frontier
buckets, with all residual mass carried by Q1 summaries. Exact-plus-residual
accounting matched every source bucket.

The arm nevertheless reached P99 206 against the frozen 192-token gate. Its
paired construction ratio was 0.909x, only 0.009x above the runtime floor.
This closes selective exact retention at the registered 16/24 limits for the
first learned comparison. The result does not justify raising the P99 gate or
quietly reducing exact-bucket limits after observing the corpus.

## Consequences

1. Keep 441 dense cells closed.
2. Keep the 61-cell focal field plus exact `CSR4AM1` overflow sidecar as the
   R4 substrate.
3. Advance Q1, Q2, and Q3 to one matched MLX comparison against exact R2.
4. Treat Q1 as the throughput-oriented candidate and Q2 as the richest
   passing candidate; do not presume either will win learned quality.
5. Retain Q4 as negative evidence and do not alter its failed threshold.
6. Continue R5 exact quotient-state controls and R6 incremental sparse
   apply/undo work as independent lanes so the cluster does not wait on one
   learned result.

## Claim Boundary

This result establishes an exact, deterministic, hard-bounded,
information-probed model view and authorizes a matched MLX comparison.

It does not establish learned decision quality, serving superiority, search
strength, gameplay strength, or progress in mean score toward 100.
