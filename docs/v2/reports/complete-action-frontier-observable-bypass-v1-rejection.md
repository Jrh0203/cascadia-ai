# Complete-Action Frontier Observable Bypass V1 Rejection

Status: **complete; rejected**

Date: 2026-06-16

Experiment ID: `complete-action-frontier-observable-bypass-v1`

## Verdict

Neither the original observable action/prior features nor those features
combined with the frozen 192-dimensional candidate embedding can fit the open
frontier target. The preregistered classification is
`observable_bypass_insufficient`.

Further candidate-wise bypass heads, output-head changes, and loss swaps are
closed. The next experiment must change how candidates receive context from
the rest of the legal action set.

The linear gate required 60% train recall and 5% exact train sets. Each
nonlinear gate required 80% train recall, 25% exact train sets, 50%
validation recall, and 1% exact validation sets. The combined probe was the
best train fit, but remained 49.50 percentage points below its recall gate and
recovered only one of 560 train target sets exactly.

The combined probe retained 77.08% of exact R4800 validation winners with
0.052483 mean retained regret. Those secondary metrics do not rescue a
representation that cannot recover its open training target.

## Throughput

The full four-host audit completed in 625.53 seconds wall-clock. The three
distinct probes launched concurrently; the longest combined probe determined
the critical path. Locally regenerating the sidecars took at most 5.84
seconds per remote host and avoided a repeated multi-gigabyte relay.

After john1 and john2 completed their probes, duplicate seeds and sweeps were
intentionally prohibited. john4 cross-replayed each artifact as it became
available. Their idle interval therefore had no compatible authorized
compute; it was not caused by scheduler neglect.

## Next Mechanism

The observable rows are unique, but a candidate-wise network still cannot
recover the target. The current trunk gives each candidate only learned
mean/max summaries of the complete action set. The next preregistered fork
must compare independent linear-memory candidate-relative mechanisms, such as
explicit group-relative statistics, richer learned set pooling, and
observable landmark context. One host should train each distinct mechanism;
replicas remain closed until a mechanism passes its train and validation
gates.
