# ADR 0182: Exact Replay Audit Before Reusing Imitation Data For O1

**Status:** Accepted and completed  
**Date:** 2026-06-16  
**Experiment:** `o1-opponent-intent-corpus-reuse-audit-v1`

## Context

O1 requires sequential opponent next-pick and post-action market-survival
labels. The existing canonical action-imitation corpus contains complete
80-turn trajectories, one selected action per state, all four public boards,
and market semantics. Its compact `PositionRecord` deliberately omits physical
`TileId`, however, so semantic snapshot matching alone cannot establish
identity survival when two standard tiles share one semantic archetype.

The omission does not automatically invalidate the corpus. Dataset split and
game index deterministically define the original hidden `GameState`, and the
selected compact action stores enough information to reconstruct the teacher's
action because this corpus prohibited paid wildlife wipes. The correct question
is therefore empirical and exact: can the complete source trajectory be replayed
from the game engine with zero state or action drift?

The train and validation manifests use the same numeric game-index interval but
different split-derived seeds. That separation must also be proven rather than
assumed.

## Decision

Before deriving any O1 labels, run a source-frozen exact replay audit over:

- `canonical-action-imitation-train-a0155b3613e51112`;
- `canonical-action-imitation-validation-4929d2a8a2bb0a0d`.

For every game, the audit must:

1. validate the manifest and every shard checksum;
2. reconstruct the initial game from `split.game_seed(game_index)`;
3. require exactly turns `0..79` and the correct active seat;
4. compare the complete stored `PositionRecord` bytes with a fresh observation;
5. reconstruct every candidate action and reproduce its BLAKE3 action hash;
6. require exactly one selected action and apply it through the rules engine;
7. reach the exact terminal game after 80 transitions;
8. recover four unique physical tile IDs at every active market;
9. quantify semantic collisions that snapshot-only matching cannot resolve;
10. derive post-focal-action survival windows across the next three opponents;
11. prove zero train/validation overlap in group IDs, compact positions, public
    states, and initial hidden states.

The primary run is assigned to john4 and an independent replay to john2. Both
use the same content-addressed executable and immutable source bundle. Their
scientific digests must match exactly.

## Survival Semantics

For a focal action at turn `t`, the relevant market is the refilled market
visible at turn `t + 1`. Each of its four unique tile IDs is tracked through
opponent actions at `t + 1`, `t + 2`, and `t + 3`, ending at the focal player's
next access at `t + 4`.

Tile survival is physical and exact because standard tile IDs are unique.
Wildlife tokens of the same species are rules-equivalent and do not have
physical IDs. Pair survival therefore means exact tile identity plus publicly
equivalent wildlife species at that tile's slot. We will not invent artificial
wildlife identity and present it as game information.

## Promotion Gates

The existing corpus is authorized as an O1 foundation only if all exact checks
pass, both splits are disjoint, and the distinct-host replay reproduces the
scientific digest.

Even a perfect replay does **not** authorize this corpus as final O1 evidence.
Both splits were generated by one teacher strategy and one checkpoint. The
final O1 corpus still requires multiple opponent policy families, policy-held-
out evaluation, strategy-switch labels where applicable, and proof that model
or checkpoint identity is unavailable to observable inputs.

## Consequences

- A passing result avoids recollecting same-policy foundation data and permits
  exact label derivation from existing trajectories.
- A failing result forces a new sequential collector with explicit market
  identity and transition labels.
- Either result removes ambiguity about the omitted 441-cell representation:
  this audit operates on compact entity records and exact game replay only.
- No gameplay or score claim follows from this audit.

## Result

The schema-v2 crossed-host replay passed under immutable bundle
`9666a78a7110056558ec608f3346046c15157f2019f12580a8976946fd4bd6b7`.
john4 primary and john2 replay reproduced scientific BLAKE3
`43e246ba50c4dac31b1d9f174acddd1ee4cbc70e14169ff7dbca254c24db0c4e`.

The existing corpus is authorized for exact same-policy O1 foundation work.
It remains unauthorized for final O1 training or promotion until a
multi-policy, policy-held-out sequential corpus exists.
